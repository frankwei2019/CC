# Benchmark Cases

Three top-performing videos with structural analysis.

---

## Benchmark 1: 欣喜若狂 (6314 plays) — `EMOTION-BURST`

### Story Structure
```
Beat 1 — Opening: 菠萝猫狂喜地敲大龙虾家门，大喊"大喜大喜啦！"
Beat 2 — Interaction: 龙虾开门说"看你欣喜若狂的，啥好事？"
Beat 3 — Spectacle: 菠萝猫表演"我要验牌"飞牌动作，
          奖状旋转飞出又飞回，转场到海边沙滩远景
Beat 4 — Dialogue: "你看号号给我发奖状了！" "太好了太好了！"
          "哈哈，你也会成语了？"
Beat 5 — Punchline: 龙虾说"看你高兴得象发狂一样，不就是欣喜若狂吗？"
```

### Why It Works
- **Strong emotional hook**: 狂喜敲门 instantly engaging
- **Internet meme integration**: "我要验牌" creates visual memory point
- **Clever punchline**: 龙虾 accidentally uses the idiom, not 菠萝猫 lecturing
- **Visual spectacle**: Flying card transition, award rotating
- **Natural dialogue**: 4 exchanges, each moving the story forward

**Best for**: idioms about strong emotions (喜、怒、哀、乐)

## Benchmark 2: 锲而不舍 — `STRUGGLE-TRIUMPH`

### Story Structure
```
Beat 1 — Opening: 菠萝猫和龙虾到海边玩，菠萝猫爬上高石头看到美景
Beat 2 — Interaction: 龙虾也想看，"我来看看！"
Beat 3 — Spectacle: 龙虾两次从石头上滚下来，摔得四脚朝天、鼻青脸肿，
          菠萝猫握拳喊"加油"
Beat 4 — Dialogue: 第三次登顶成功 "这里真是太美了！"
          "真是锲而不舍，金石可镂啊！"
Beat 5 — Punchline: 菠萝猫解释"你为了看风景，摔了又爬，坚持不懈地努力，
          就是锲而不舍！"
```

### Why It Works
- **Physical comedy**: Falling, flipping over kids find funny and tense
- **Relatable struggle**: Can't climb up every child has experienced this
- **Tension-release**: Two failures third success emotional payoff
- **Action teaches the lesson**: 龙虾's experience = living demo of the idiom
- **Positive ending**: Perseverance leads to seeing the beautiful view

**Best for**: idioms about perseverance, effort, growth

## Benchmark 3: 喜极而泣 — `PARADOX-REVEAL`

### Story Structure
```
Beat 1 — Opening: 菠萝猫跑到大龙虾家门口，没敲门，放声大哭
Beat 2 — Interaction: 大龙虾听到哭声开门，双手抱着菠萝猫的头关切问"怎么啦？"
Beat 3 — Spectacle: 菠萝猫继续哭，嘴巴张得象哆啦A梦一样大，
          蓝色大颗眼泪飞溅，闪闪发光，地上汇成一片
Beat 4 — Dialogue: "昨天单个成语播放量都超过1万了！"
          "那你应该高兴，怎么反而哭了呢？"
Beat 5 — Punchline: 菠萝猫说"因为太高兴、太激动而哭，这是喜极而泣"
```

### Why It Works
- **Paradox hook**: Happy but crying? creates curiosity
- **Anime-style exaggeration**: Doraemon-style crying, blue tears splashing visual impact
- **Emotional contrast**: Exaggerated crying + good news =反差萌
- **Visual spectacle**: Tears splashing on 龙虾, pooling on the ground, as blue as the sea
- **Natural explanation**: 菠萝猫 explains herself, no narrator

**Best for**: idioms with surprising/contradictory meanings

## Universal 5-Beat Template

```
Beat 1 — OPENING (0-3s)
菠萝猫 arrives with strong emotion/action
→ Must establish emotional hook in first 1 second

Beat 2 — INTERACTION (3-5s)
大龙虾 responds — curiosity / concern / challenge / doubt
→ Establishes dialogue relationship

Beat 3 — SPECTACLE (5-10s)
Exaggerated physical action + concrete visual details
→ Match specificity of "我要验牌" "四脚朝天" "蓝眼泪飞溅"

Beat 4 — DIALOGUE (overlaps spectacle)
3-5 colloquial exchanges, naturally leading to the idiom
→ Characters talk like real people, not lecturers

Beat 5 — PUNCHLINE (10-15s)
Character explains/uses the idiom through dialogue
→ 菠萝猫 or 大龙虾 says the idiom as part of conversation
```

## Story Angle Quality Checklist

For each generated angle, check:
- [ ] Does the opening create an instant emotional hook?
- [ ] Is the visual spectacle as specific as the benchmarks?
- [ ] Does the dialogue feel like real conversation?
- [ ] Is the idiom explained through dialogue, not narration?
- [ ] Is there a clear benchmark pattern match?
- [ ] Would a child laugh or lean in?
